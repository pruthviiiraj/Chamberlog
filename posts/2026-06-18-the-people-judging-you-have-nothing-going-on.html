The People Judging You Have Nothing Going On

There is a pattern in who criticises and who creates. They are almost never the same people.

Research on the "superstar effect" shows that the people most vocal in judging others' work are structurally uninformative. They are not offering useful data about your performance. They are projecting their own inaction. The criticism is permanent, meaning it will be there regardless of what you do, and it is irrelevant, meaning it carries no information about whether your work is good or whether you should change course.

This matters for performance because fear of judgment is one of the most effective brakes on action. It feels like a real risk, like there is an audience keeping score, and their verdict matters. But the verdict was written before you started. The people keeping score from the sidelines are wearing someone else's jersey. Recognising this does not make the fear vanish. It does remove the false premise that the fear is protecting you from something real. What it is actually protecting you from is the discomfort of being visible. And visibility is the prerequisite for every meaningful thing you want to do.
