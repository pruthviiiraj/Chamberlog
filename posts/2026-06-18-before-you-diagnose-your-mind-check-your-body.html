Before You Diagnose Your Mind, Check Your Body

The anxiety you are trying to think your way out of might be dehydration.

Hunger, poor sleep, and dehydration do not announce themselves as physical problems. They disguise themselves as emotional narratives. You skip lunch and by 3pm you are convinced your career is going nowhere. You sleep five hours and wake up certain that nobody cares about you. The body is generating a stress signal, and your brain, which needs a story to explain every feeling, writes one. The story feels true because the feeling is real. But the feeling is not caused by the story. It is caused by the body.

This is not a metaphor. Cortisol rises when blood sugar drops. Serotonin production (90-95% of which happens in the gut) shifts with what you ate yesterday. One night of poor sleep makes you as insulin resistant as a pre-diabetic, which means your brain is literally underfueled despite having food available. The emotional narrative you constructed around those states, "I am unmotivated," "I am anxious," "I am stuck," was never the diagnosis. It was the symptom wearing a mask.

The fix is embarrassingly simple and worth trying before any deeper analysis: eat, drink water, sleep. If the feeling persists after the body is stable, then it is real and worth investigating. If it vanishes, it was never yours. It was your body talking, and you were listening to the wrong translation.
