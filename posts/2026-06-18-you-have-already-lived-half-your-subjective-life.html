You Have Already Lived Half Your Subjective Life

If you are 25, you have already experienced roughly half of your felt lifetime, regardless of how many years the calendar promises you.

Time perception is not linear. Childhood summers lasted forever because everything was novel. Your brain encoded each new experience as a distinct memory, and when you look back, the density of those memories makes the period feel long. After 25, novelty thins out. Routines replace firsts. Years begin to blur into each other because your brain has fewer distinct events to mark them by. The subjective midpoint of a 80-year life is not 40. It is somewhere around 25.

This reframes "someday" from a reasonable plan into an already-late one. The urgency most people feel in their thirties, the sense that time is accelerating, is not anxiety. It is an accurate perception of a real phenomenon. The years after 25 contain less felt time per calendar year than the years before. If there is something you have been meaning to start, the window in which it will feel like there is plenty of time has already closed. What remains is still enough. But "later" is a smaller container than it looks from the outside.
