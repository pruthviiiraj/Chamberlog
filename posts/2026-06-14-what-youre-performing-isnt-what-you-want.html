What you're performing and what you actually want aren't the same thing

Blanche Ingram had everything society considered impressive. Rochester looked at her and felt nothing.

Both of the sharpest people in the room, Jane Eyre and Rochester himself, looked at Blanche's polished performance and saw something missing. No genuine warmth. No real sympathy. No authentic character. What she had was the performance of these qualities, carried off with skill. People who are themselves performing tend to be impressed by performance in others. People with genuine depth detect the absence immediately. Polished surfaces work on audiences who don't know what to look for. They don't work on the people whose regard is actually worth having.

The same structure runs through a surprising number of high-achieving people: a belief, usually learned in childhood, that love is something you earn by performing well enough. The child who received warmth only when they succeeded learned early, at a level too deep to easily see, that achievement is how you get love. So they become an adult who keeps achieving, always waiting for an approval that will finally feel unconditional. But they've also, without realizing it, selected people around them who reflect the belief back. The harder you work to earn love through achievement, the more it confirms that love requires earning. Any relationship that conditions itself on your performance isn't love. It's a transaction.

The Bhagavad Gita, an ancient Hindu text about how to act with integrity, names the mechanism that produces both patterns. Once your motivation shifts from doing the thing to getting something for the thing, the deeper satisfaction becomes structurally unavailable. You can no longer access it. The satisfaction that comes from acting out of genuine conviction and the satisfaction of getting what you wanted are different in kind, not just degree. One rises and falls with outcomes. The other is constant, because the action is the thing, not what it produces. Genuine character is the one quality that can't be manufactured indefinitely, and can't be faked in front of people who possess it themselves. Every other accomplishment can be acquired. This one can't.
