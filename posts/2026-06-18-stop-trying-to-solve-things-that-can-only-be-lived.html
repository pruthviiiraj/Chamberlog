Stop Trying to Solve Things That Can Only Be Lived

You cannot debug a sunset. And you cannot optimise your way out of loneliness.

There are two types of hard problems. Complicated problems have parts, mechanisms, and solutions. A car engine is complicated. With enough knowledge and effort, you crack it. Complex problems are alive. A marriage is complex. Grief is complex. Your relationship with your own ambition is complex. These cannot be solved because they are not puzzles. They are realities to live inside of.

The category error most people make is treating complex problems as if they were complicated. Dating apps engineer love. Self-help frameworks try to process grief to completion. Productivity systems try to manufacture meaning. None of these work because the target belongs to a different category. The tools that solve complicated problems (analysis, measurement, optimisation) actually make complex problems worse, because they give you the sensation of progress while blocking the surrender that complexity requires. The irony is that the moment you stop trying to solve the complex problem, it often begins to shift. Not because surrender is magic. Because the engineering was the interference.
